Orders Of Magnitude

... are a way to define the size of the universe

They are measured in powers of ten, and range from

10^-35 to 10^26. When you get to 10^0, you are at

exactly one meter. Every step you go 10 times higher

or 10 times lower. We start at the smallest thing possible...

10^-35

Planck's constant

Planck's Constant is the smallest any possible thing in the
universe can be. It is 100 quintillion times smaller than one meter.
That's 1/100,000,000,000,000,000,000th of a meter!

10^-24

1 septillionth of a meter!

The size of an electron!

Electrons are tiny particles that orbit the nucleus of an atom
in a sort of "probability cloud". (Quantum physics are really weird)

10^-21 - 10^-18

1 sextillionth - 1 quintillionth of a meter

The size of quarks

Quarks build protons and neutrons, which are part of atomic nuclei
They create the fundamental building blocks for all matter. There are
6 types of quarks: Up, Down, Top, Bottom, Strange, and Charm.

10^-15

1 quadrillionth of a meter

The size of a single proton

Protons are made of quarks, and are one of 2 parts of atomic
nuclei. They are the building blocks of the building blocks
of the universe.

10^-14

Ten quadrillionths of a meter

The size of an atomic nucleus

The nucleus is the center of an atom, it is made of protons and
neutrons. The biggest atom has 118 protons in the nucleus.

10^-13

100 quadrillionths of a meter

Atomic emptiness

Atomic emptiness is the space between the electron orbitals
and the nucleus of atoms. Some very heavy man-made atoms
fall into this size range

10^-12

1 trillionth of a meter

Minerals in Neutron Stars

Extremely heavily compressed atoms and their electron orbitals
fit into this size range. One teaspoon of neutron star matter
weighs 4 billion tons (8,000,000,000,000 pounds)

10^-11

10 trillionths of a meter

Interior orbitals of a carbon atom

The inside orbitals of a normal carbon atom. Carbon
is essential for all water based life on Earth!

10^-10

100 trillionths of a meter

Water molecule

I'm not gonna explain what water is. Come on.
You know what water is.

10^-9

One billionth of a meter

Complex molecules

Most complex molecules will fall into this size range
Hemoglobin, which is in our red blood cells, falls
into this category

10^-8

10 billionths of a meter

Width of chromosomes

Chromosomes help make there is enough DNA during cell
divisions and they help to determine your gender at birth.

10^-7

100 billionths of a meter

Viruses

Virus forms are usually around this size. One notable virus
is called COVID-19, you may not have heard of it.
(that sentence was dripping with sarcasm)

10^-6

1 millionth of a meter (One micrometer)

Large bacteria

Some large bacteria are around this size (one micrometer)
You probably know what bacteria are.

10^-5

10 micrometers

Most animal cells

The cells of most animals will fit into this size range
Mushroom spores and cactus pollen would as well.

10^-4

0.1 millimeters

Plant cells/pollen

Most plant cells come in this size range becuase of their thick
cell walls. Plant pollens come in at this range too.

10^-3

1 millimeter

The size of a snowflake

Most dendritic snowflakes (The ones with cool patterns)
are in this size range. This is where you can begin to see
things well with your eyes.

10^-2

1 centimeter

The size of a lego brick

1x1 or 1x2 lego bricks are the closest to being this size,
but most legos do fall into this range.

10^-1

1 decimeter (10 centimeters)

The size of a baseball

An apple, a pear, a baseball, and things around that size
are close to one decimeter in size.

10^0

1 meter

The size of a small child

One meter. The building block of every order of magnitude,
from the biggest to the smallest.
