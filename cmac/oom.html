Orders Of Magnitude

... are a way to define the size of the universe

They are measured in powers of ten, and range from

10^-35 to 10^26. When you get to 10^0, you are at

exactly one meter. Every step you go 10 times higher

or 10 times lower. We start at the smallest thing possible...

10^-35

Planck's constant

Planck's Constant is the smallest any possible thing in the
universe can be. It is 100 quintillion times smaller than one meter.
That's 1/100,000,000,000,000,000,000th of a meter!

10^-24

1 septillionth of a meter!

The size of an electron!

Electrons are tiny particles that orbit the nucleus of an atom
in a sort of "probability cloud". (Quantum physics are really weird)

10^-21 - 10^-18

1 sextillionth - 1 quintillionth of a meter

The size of quarks

Quarks build protons and neutrons, which are part of atomic nuclei
They create the fundamental building blocks for all matter. There are
6 types of quarks: Up, Down, Top, Bottom, Strange, and Charm.

10^-15

1 quadrillionth of a meter

The size of a single proton

Protons are made of quarks, and are one of 2 parts of atomic
nuclei. They are the building blocks of the building blocks
of the universe.

10^-14

Ten quadrillionths of a meter

The size of an atomic nucleus

The nucleus is the center of an atom, it is made of protons and
neutrons. The biggest atom has 118 protons in the nucleus.

10^-13

100 quadrillionths of a meter

Atomic emptiness

Atomic emptiness is the space between the electron orbitals
and the nucleus of atoms. Some very heavy man-made atoms
fall into this size range

10^-12

1 trillionth of a meter

Minerals in Neutron Stars

Extremely heavily compressed atoms and their electron orbitals
fit into this size range. One teaspoon of neutron star matter
weighs 4 billion tons (8,000,000,000,000 pounds)

10^-11

10 trillionths of a meter

Interior orbitals of a carbon atom

The inside orbitals of a normal carbon atom. Carbon
is essential for all water based life on Earth!

10^-10

100 trillionths of a meter

Water molecule

I'm not gonna explain what water is. Come on.
You know what water is.

10^-9

One billionth of a meter

Complex molecules

Most complex molecules will fall into this size range
Hemoglobin, which is in our red blood cells, falls
into this category

10^-8

10 billionths of a meter

Width of chromosomes

Chromosomes help make there is enough DNA during cell
divisions and they help to determine your gender at birth.

10^-7

100 billionths of a meter

Viruses

Virus forms are usually around this size. One notable virus
is called COVID-19, you may not have heard of it.
(that sentence was dripping with sarcasm)

10^-6

1 millionth of a meter (One micrometer)

Large bacteria

Some large bacteria are around this size (one micrometer)
You probably know what bacteria are.

10^-5

10 micrometers

Most animal cells

The cells of most animals will fit into this size range
Mushroom spores and cactus pollen would as well.

10^-4

0.1 millimeters

Plant cells/pollen

Most plant cells come in this size range becuase of their thick
cell walls. Plant pollens come in at this range too.

10^-3

1 millimeter

The size of a snowflake

Most dendritic snowflakes (The ones with cool patterns)
are in this size range. This is where you can begin to see
things well with your eyes.

10^-2

1 centimeter

The size of a lego brick

1x1 or 1x2 lego bricks are the closest to being this size,
but most legos do fall into this range.

10^-1

1 decimeter (10 centimeters)

The size of a baseball

An apple, a pear, a baseball, and things around that size
are close to one decimeter in size.

10^0

1 meter

The size of a small child

One meter. The building block of every order of magnitude,
from the biggest to the smallest.

10^1

10 meters

Cars

Most cars are around this size, even limousines.
Most houses are also in this order of magnitude.

10^2

100 meters

Giant trees

Some trees can get up to this height, such as the
General Sequoia Sherman. Usain Bolt can run this far in ~9 seconds.

10^3

1 kilometer

Golden Gate Bridge

The Golden Gate Bridge is on the higher end of this range. An average
person can walk this far in 7 minutes.

10^4

10 kilometers

Small towns

Most small towns are about 10 kilometers long.
Cold fronts are aso about the smae size.

10^5

100 kilometers

Ireland

Ireland (Yes, THAT Ireland) is about 100 kilometers across. That's about
twice the distance an eagle will fly to catch prey.

10^6

1 thousand kilometers

Mexico

Mexico is about his size, or the state of California
Brazil could be in this size range.

10^7

10 thousand kilometers

Earth.

All of Earth.

10^8

100 thousand kilometers

Jupiter

If Jupiter were the size of a basketball, Earth would be the relative size of
a grape. Yeah.

10^9

1 million kilometers

Our Sun

If we kept that model where Earth is a grape, the Sun would be a giant beach ball
4 feet across.

10^10

10 million kilometers

Large stars

Many stars are near this size, like Arcturus, Polaris, and Delta Boötes

10^11

100 million kilometers

Most red giant stars

A red giant star is a star that is almost dead, and is going to explode in
a supernova soon. They are 100-1000 times wider than our sun.

10^12

1 billion kilometers

Size of Jupiter's orbit

How wide the orbit of Jupiter is as it revolved around the sun. For context,
this is 1,000 times bigger than the sun.

10^13

10 billion kilometers

Pluto's orbit

Pluto goes this far away from the sun in a weird elliptical pattern unlike
most planetary orbits.

10^14

100 billion kilometers

Orbit of most comets

Most cometary orbits take them within visible distance of the Earth only
once about every 100 years or more. The most famous of these is probably
Halley's Comet.

10^15

1 trillion kilometers

Cat's Eye Nebula

Nebulae are factories that make new stars. They are comprised of basic gases
such as hydrogen and helium.

10^16

1 light-year (10 trillion kilometers)

Ring Nebula

Look above for what a nebula is :)
10 trillion kilometers is also called a light year.

10^17

10 light-years

Pleiades Cluster

The Pleiades Cluster is made up of lots of stars
It is about 444 light years away from Earth.

10^18

100 light-years

Distance to the main stars of the Big Dipper

The distance to the main stars in the Big Dipper constellation is
about 80 light-years away.

10^19

1 thousand light-years

Distance to Betelgeuse/Rigel

These are two of the main stars in the Orion Constellation.
They are a red supergiant and a blue-white supergiant, respectively.

10^20

10 thousand light-years

The size of the Mice Galaxies

The Mice galaxies are smaller than our Milky Way, but they are still
about 100 quintillion meters across!

10^21

100 thousand light-years

The Milky Way

the Milky Way is our home galaxy, there are about 100-400 stars in it.
The center of the galaxy is the supermassive black hole Sagittarius A*.

10^22

1 million light-years

Local Group

The Local Group is all of the galaxies nearby us, this includes things like Andromeda
and it's satellite galaxies and the Milky Way's satellites

10^23

10 million light-years

Virgo Supercluster

The Virgo Supercluster contains many groups of galaxies like our Local Group
and the M81 Group.

10^24

100 million light-years

Galactic Voids

These are the spaces between clusters/groups/superclusters in the Universe. The vast
majority of all the space in the Universe is completely and utterly empty.

10^25

1 billion light-years

Great Wall Of Galaxies

The Great Wall Of Galaxies is a giant area filled with galxies, like if you splattered paint all over
something.

10^26

10 billion light-years

The Universe
